Success Criterion 1.4.6: Contrast (Enhanced) (Level AAA)

The visual presentation of text and images of text has a contrast ratio of at least 7:1, except for the following:

  • Large Text: Large-scale text and images of large-scale text have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1;
  • Incidental: Text or images of text that are part of an inactive user interface component, that are pure decoration, that are not visible to anyone, or that are part of a picture that contains significant other visual content, have no contrast requirement.
  • Logotypes: Text that is part of a logo or brand name has no minimum contrast requirement.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 1.4.6)

Discussion

Summary

This is the stricter version of a two-stage criterion relating to contrast between foreground and background colours; the AAA version exists as Success Criterion 1.4.3; please see that document for background information regarding contrast and luminance.

What changes in this version?

The minimum contrast ratio for applicable text, or images of text, is increased by a further 1.5 times; for body text the minimum becomes 7:1, while a 4.5:1 ratio is required for large-scale text.

This is nominated to satisfy the requirements of users who have as low as 20/80 vision. Beyond this point, it is likely that users will be relying on assistive technology to view content.

Otherwise, the same exceptions and relaxations apply as Success Criterion 1.4.3.
