Success Criterion 3.2.4: Consistent Identification (Level AA)

Components that have the same functionality within a set of Web pages are identified consistently.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 3.2.4)

Discussion

Summary

This Success Criterion, one of the "Predictable" guideline criteria, relates to the consistent labelling of functionality. Design elements that perform the same function should be consistently labelled.

Consistent doesn't imply completely "identical". For instance, a link going to the next page could simply say "Next Page", or contain some other piece of identifying information ("Go to Page 2", "Next Page: [Title of Page 2]" or similar). The labels, however, are consistent between instances, and thus predictable.

Different simple design elements, such as ticks and crosses, may mean different things in different contexts, although they should be marked up with text alternatives as appropriate to their situation. However, where they have the same meaning, they should have the same text alternative.
