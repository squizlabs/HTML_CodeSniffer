Success Criterion 3.1.3: Unusual Words (Level AAA)

A mechanism is available for identifying specific definitions of words or phrases used in an unusual or restricted way, including idioms and jargon.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 3.1.3)

Discussion

Summary

The purpose of the Level AAA Success Criteria in Guideline 3.1 is to ensure that the content contained within is as easy to understand for as wide a range of people as possible.

This Success Criterion intends to reduce confusion for some users by ensuring that words used in a specialised or unusual way are minimised, or at least explained. For jargon, this could be done by explaining it directly after the word, or providing a glossary which can be linked to.

This Success Criterion also addresses phrases considered idioms, where the meaning of a phrase differs from its potential literal reading - the Understanding document gives the example of the English phrase "to spill the beans" as not literally meaning spilling beans, but rather to reveal a closely-held secret. Use of such phrases is discouraged because they may not be understood well by those with a limited grasp of the language (including as a second language), as well as some groups of people who may have difficulty reading it for something other than its literal meaning.
