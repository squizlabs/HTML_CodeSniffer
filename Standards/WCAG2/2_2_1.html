Success Criterion 2.2.1: Non-text Content (Level A)

For each time limit that is set by the content, at least one of the following is true:

  • Turn off: The user is allowed to turn off the time limit before encountering it; or
  • Adjust: The user is allowed to adjust the time limit before encountering it over a wide range that is at least ten times the length of the default setting; or
  • Extend: The user is warned before time expires and given at least 20 seconds to extend the time limit with a simple action (for example, "press the space bar"), and the user is allowed to extend the time limit at least ten times; or
  • Real-time Exception: The time limit is a required part of a real-time event (for example, an auction), and no alternative to the time limit is possible; or
  • Essential Exception: The time limit is essential and extending it would invalidate the activity; or
  • 20 Hour Exception: The time limit is longer than 20 hours.

Note: This success criterion helps ensure that users can complete tasks without unexpected changes in content or context that are a result of a time limit. This success criterion should be considered in conjunction with Success Criterion 3.2.1, which puts limits on changes of content or context as a result of user action.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 2.2.1)

Summary

The purpose of this Success Criterion is to make sure that timed interaction in Web pages is not designed solely with able-bodied persons in mind. Those that may need additional time include users with physical as well as cognitive disabilities.

At AAA level, the stricter Success Criterion 2.2.3 applies. There, the only reason for allowing timing for an interaction is the "real-time" exception.

Methods of satisfying

Three methods are provided for satisfying this Success Criterion, of which one must be provided, unless an exception applies:

  • The user can turn off the time limit.
  • The user has the ability to change the time limit to something at least 10 times the default.
  • The user is warned at least 20 seconds before the time limit expires and told to perform a simple action (eg. "press any key") to extend, and can do this at least 10 times.

It appears that any extension should reset the time limit to the originally set value, given how the Adjust and Extend solutions align with each other, but this is not written in the document.

The "10 times default" and 20 second time periods are based on clinical evidence. The latter gives sufficient time for most users to hit any key (or the space bar or similar), but is considered short enough as to not to put users at risk of snooping or other security breaches.

Exceptions

Three exceptions exist in this Success Criterion that allow you to avoid the requirement to allow a user to turn off, adjust or extend the time limit:

  • The interaction relates to a real-time activity, and this timing cannot be avoided (for instance, an online auction).
  • The interaction is essential to the activity (see below for definition).
  • The initial time period is greater than 20 hours; chosen because it is longer than a typical full day awake.

An "essential" interaction is defined as one that

if removed, would fundamentally change the information or functionality of the content, and information and functionality cannot be achieved in another way that would conform

WCAG 2.0 Recommendation: "Essential" definition

An example given by the Understanding document is of a ticketing web site, that only holds tickets for a specified period at each stage (say, 5-15 minutes) before timing out. This is justified as part of a ticket management process for high-demand events, and allowing users to extend the time-out period would invalidate this business process. So in this case, at least part of the process is considered "essential".

Note, however, that not all parts of a process may be considered "essential". For instance, such a site could allow a user, who would generally have advance knowledge of an event, to register with the web site in their own time, or before tickets are held.
