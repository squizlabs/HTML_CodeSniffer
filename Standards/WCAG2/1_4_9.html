Success Criterion 1.4.9: Images of Text (No Exception) (Level AAA)

Images of text are only used for pure decoration or where a particular presentation of text is essential to the information being conveyed.

Note: Logotypes (text that is part of a logo or brand name) are considered essential.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 1.4.9)

Discussion

Summary

This is a stricter version of Success Criterion 1.4.5, which operates at AA level. See that success criterion for further discussion, as the techniques involved are the same, only with less exceptions.

Which exceptions apply?

In Success Criterion 1.4.9, only a small number of exceptions exist. Excuses such as not being able to achieve the required visual presentation do not cut it at the Triple A level.

Exceptions include the following situations:

  • The image of text is considered "essential". This includes items such as branding, font samples, or other items where the original format is essential to the meaning - the Understanding document provides the example of a historic original letter. In these circumstances, a text alternative (short or long as appropriate) would still be appropriate.

  • The image of text is considered "purely decorative". If you would put an empty short text alternative to the image of text, then it could be exempted. This should not occur often.

  • The image of text is not an "image of text" after all. Images of text "[do] not include text that is part of a picture that contains significant other visual content", such as a graph, screenshot, or other diagram.
