Success Criterion 1.2.9: Audio-only (Live) (Level AAA)

An alternative for time-based media that presents equivalent information for live audio-only content is provided.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 1.2.9)

Discussion

Definitions

For explanation of the definitions of real-time media used in the description of this Success Criterion, please see the discussion section of Success Criterion 1.2.1.

What is required?

This Success Criterion requires some form of text alternative. If a script is provided beforehand, and the script is kept to, a transcript of the live audio can be used. However, the preferred method is to provide a live captioning service, operated by a trained captioner:

They are able to capture a live event with a high degree of fidelity, and also to insert notes on any non spoken audio which is essential to understanding the event[...] a live caption service is preferred because it plays out at the same pace as the audio itself, and can adapt to any deviations from the script that might occur.

Using untrained operators, or providing a transcript which differs markedly from what actually happens would not be considered meeting this Success Criterion.
