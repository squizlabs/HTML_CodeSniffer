Success Criterion 1.3.3: Sensory Characteristics (Level A)

Instructions provided for understanding and operating content do not rely solely on sensory characteristics of components such as shape, size, visual location, orientation, or sound.

Note: For requirements related to color, refer to Guideline 1.4.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 1.3.3)

Discussion

Summary

This Success Criterion exists so that users with sensory deficiencies, such as reduced ability to determine size, shape or location, are able to access content, by providing instructions that do not solely rely on descriptions that may be outside their sense of perception.

It does not suggest that sensory descriptions be completely removed from instructions - as they can help users with other cognitive deficiencies (such as reading difficulties) - but that they not be the sole description. For instance, the instruction...

When finished, click the big button at the bottom of the page.

...would not satisfy this Success Criterion. It depends on being able to sense size and location, which not all users would be able to do. However...

When finished, click the big button at the bottom of the page, labelled 'Submit Form'.

...would be perfectly fine, if long-winded. An alternative has been provided - the name of the button - which can be used by users who cannot grasp what is "big" or where the "bottom" of the page is.

Note that some locations, like "select one option below", may not necessarily fail this Success Criterion, at least in English:

In some languages, it is commonly understood that "above" refers to the content previous to that point in the content and "below" refers to the content after that point. In such languages, if the content being referenced is in the appropriate place in the reading order and the references are unambiguous, statements such as "choose one of the links below" or "all of the above" would conform to this Success Criterion.
