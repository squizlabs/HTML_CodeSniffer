Success Criterion 1.4.8: Visual Presentation (Level AAA)

For the visual presentation of blocks of text, a mechanism is available to achieve the following:

  • Foreground and background colors can be selected by the user.
  • Width is no more than 80 characters or glyphs (40 if CJK).
  • Text is not justified (aligned to both the left and the right margins).
  • Line spacing (leading) is at least space-and-a-half within paragraphs, and paragraph spacing is at least 1.5 times larger than the line spacing.
  • Text can be resized without assistive technology up to 200 percent in a way that does not require the user to scroll horizontally to read a line of text on a full-screen window.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 1.4.8)

Discussion

Summary

  • Foreground and background colors can be selected by the user.

    Some users require the ability to set colours and other presentation of text as they require it, for various reasons. At the very least, changes in presentation should be possible through the use of a user style sheet.

  • *Width is no more than 80 characters or glyphs (40 if CJK).

    Shorter line lengths in blocks of text can improve readability for those with some reading or vision disabilities. Asian scripts mentioned above usually use double-width characters, so the maximum number of glyphs per line is halved for text in those scripts.

  • Text is not justified (aligned to both the left and the right margins).

    Uneven spacing can make text more difficult to read. In CSS, blocks of text should not use the text-align: justify setting.

  • Line spacing (leading) is at least space-and-a-half within paragraphs, and paragraph spacing is at least 1.5 times larger than the line spacing.

    Extra spacing between lines and paragraphs make it easier to track them, being especially helpful to users with cognitive difficulties but also useful for all sighted readers.

    "Space and a half" means an extra. With typical normal CSS line-spacing being 1.2em (120% of the font size) in most browsers, this suggests a minimum line-spacing setting of 1.8em to satisfy this criterion.

  • Text can be resized without assistive technology up to 200 percent in a way that does not require the user to scroll horizontally to read a line of text on a full-screen window.

    This does not mean that seeing a horizontal scroll bar at 200% zoom automatically fails the success criterion, because it only applies to a specific block of text. For instance, multiple columns of text can cause the horizontal scroll bar without failure, as long as one does not need to scroll back and forth to read a single specific column.
