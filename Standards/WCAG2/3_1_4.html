Success Criterion 3.1.4: Abbreviations (Level AAA)

A mechanism for identifying the expanded form or meaning of abbreviations is available.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 3.1.4)

Discussion

Summary

The purpose of the Level AAA Success Criteria in Guideline 3.1 is to ensure that the content contained within is as easy to understand for as wide a range of people as possible.

This Success Criterion aims to ensure users can access the expanded form of abbreviations and acronyms, which may not always be obvious, and probably should not be assumed to be so. For instance, it would not be immediately obvious that "WCAG" in this context refers to "Web Content Accessibility Guidelines". It should be expanded at least in the first occurrence of , or tagged using the abbr element (or equivalent tagging methods in other web technologies).

Also possible are abbreviations that have multiple meanings: for instance saying that "St Mary's Cathedral in Sydney is located on College St". The "St" abbreviation is ambiguous in this sense, and adding definitions or abbr elements around them help disambiguate the two.

Abbr vs. acronym element

In HTML 4.01, both abbr and acronym elements were available to mark "abbreviated forms" and acronyms, respectly. In HTML5, there is only the abbr element, and acronym has been made non-conforming. As HTML 4.01 does not make it sufficiently clear, HTML5 states that the title attribute of an acronym should contain the expansion of the abbreviation and nothing else.

Any further description of what the abbreviation means is likely relevant to all users - probably qualifying the term as jargon under Success Criterion 3.1.3 - and should be explained in other ways, not in the title attribute of the abbreviation.
