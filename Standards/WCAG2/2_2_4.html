Success Criterion 2.2.4: Interruptions (Level AAA)

Interruptions can be postponed or suppressed by the user, except interruptions involving an emergency.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 2.2.4)

Discussion

Summary

This is a stricter application of part of Success Criterion 2.2.2. It mainly relates to auto-updating in relation to that Success Criterion, but also deals with alerts and other interruptions in general, and their application to users with cognitive disabilities, such as attention deficit disorder.

Where an alert or update occurs, this Success Criterion asks that it can be suppressed by the user in some way. This allows such users to focus on the content, and reduce the level of misunderstanding resulting from starting in one item of content and ending up in another.

An alert would have to be rather serious to be considered an "emergency", which is defined in the WCAG 2.0 definitions as:

a sudden, unexpected situation or occurrence that requires immediate action to preserve health, safety, or property
