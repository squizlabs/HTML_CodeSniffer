Success Criterion 1.4.3: Contrast (Minimum) (Level AA)

The visual presentation of text and images of text has a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1, except for the following:

  • Large Text: Large-scale text and images of large-scale text have a contrast ratio of at least 3:1;
  • Incidental: Text or images of text that are part of an inactive user interface component, that are pure decoration, that are not visible to anyone, or that are part of a picture that contains significant other visual content, have no contrast requirement.
  • Logotypes: Text that is part of a logo or brand name has no minimum contrast requirement.

(From Understanding WCAG 2.0: Understanding Success Criterion 1.4.3)

Note

Superseded by Success Criterion 1.4.6 at level AAA, which is a stricter application of the same concept.

Summary

This is the minimum version of a two-stage criterion relating to contrast between foreground and background colours; the AAA version exists as Success Criterion 1.4.6. This Success Criterion applies both to text and images of text.

The W3C documentation explains the reasoning for the above ratios. The 3:1 ratio is a ergonomic standard under ANSI and ISO (ISO 9241), recommended for normal observers with nominal 20/20 visual acuity.

The Understanding SC 1.2.6 document cites a study which suggests an empirical finding that the difference between 20/20 and 20/40 visual acuity includes a loss of contrast sensitivity, such that a 1.5x increase in contrast is required to remain perceptible. Hence, the minimum contrast for body text is set to 4.5:1.

This is stated to provide adequate contrast to those who do not use assistive technology to enhance contrast, but regardless have moderately low vision.

Contrast ratio calcuation

The contrast ratio used requires the relative luminance of the two colours to be calculated; the W3C provides a formula for calculating relative luminance. This provides a value between 0.0 (black) and 1.0 (white). A constant (of 0.05) is added as an adjustment for average ambient light, and the two are divided.

The minimum possible ratio is 1:1 (the same colour), while the maximum possible is 21:1 (between white and black).

Exceptions

Exceptions are made for the following:

  • Large-scale text, including images thereof. In Latin-based script, "large text" is defined as 18-point normal text, or 14-point bold text (usually 120% and 150% of the default body text size, respectively). These have a relaxed contrast requirement of 3:1.

  • Text or images that are incidental, that is

    part of an inactive user interface component, that are pure decoration, that are not visible to anyone, or that are part of a picture that contains significant other visual content, have no contrast requirement.

    These have no minimum contrast requirement.

  • Text that is part of a logotype. These have no minimum contrast requirement.
